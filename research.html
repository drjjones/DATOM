Product Vision Research Waitlist

Research foundations

DATOM treats confidence as a property of evidence structure, not model output.

The research goal is practical: make reasoning inspectable so teams can validate fast, disagree clearly, and improve safely. DATOM does this by representing claims as discrete units and making relationships explicit.

The result is a system where confidence is defendable—because it is traceable to evidence, shaped by replication, and penalized by credible contradictions.

Core idea

Most AI systems turn evidence into latent representations and then produce text that is hard to audit. DATOM keeps evidence explicit and builds a structured layer above it, so reasoning can be examined and improved.

Claims as assessable units

A claim is stored as something you can point to, review, test, and refine—rather than a sentence embedded in a chat log.

Relationships between claims

Agreement, contradiction, and gaps are first-class. This makes it possible to see where a conclusion is strong, weak, or unknown.

Confidence as a computable signal

Confidence derives from the shape and quality of evidence: completeness, density, replication, and credible counterevidence.

Why it matters

Stakeholders need different things from AI: speed, accountability, safety, and repeatability. DATOM is designed so the same underlying structures can serve all of them.

For leadership

A clearer risk posture: what the system knows, what it doesn’t, and what would change the answer.

For engineering

Faster iteration with fewer regressions: you can see which evidence supports a behavior and update it deliberately.

For audit and compliance

A reviewable trail: conclusions map to evidence units and relationships that can be inspected without relying on model authority.

How confidence is built

Confidence should rise when evidence is complete and independently replicated, and fall when high-quality contradictions appear. DATOM is structured around that intuition so updates are rational and auditable.

Evidence quality

Confidence increases when claims are grounded in clear source material and captured with enough detail to assess.

Replication strength

Independent confirmations raise confidence, especially when they agree under similar constraints and methods.

Contradiction handling

Credible counterevidence reduces confidence in a targeted way, preventing overconfidence and highlighting what needs resolution.

Research direction

The focus is to make structured evidence usable in real systems: ingestion that preserves sources, extraction that produces reliable claim units, and reasoning that stays legible when models change.

Extraction with accountability

Claims must remain tied to their provenance so errors are detectable and correctable, rather than silently propagated.

Reasoning that stays stable over time

When new evidence arrives, the system should update in ways that are explainable and consistent with the evidence structure.

Human + machine collaboration

Experts should be able to challenge, refine, and extend evidence structures without redoing work or losing auditability.
