Research foundations

DATOM treats confidence as a property of evidence structure, not model output.

The research goal is practical: make reasoning inspectable so teams can validate fast, disagree clearly, and improve safely. DATOM does this by representing claims as discrete units and making relationships explicit.

The result is a system where confidence is defendable—because it is traceable to evidence, shaped by replication, and penalized by credible contradictions.

Core idea

Most AI systems produce text that is hard to audit. DATOM keeps evidence explicit and builds a structured layer above it, so reasoning can be examined and improved.

Claims as assessable units

A claim is stored as a DATOM—something you can review and test, rather than a sentence in a chat log.

Relationships between claims

Agreement, contradiction, and gaps are first-class, making it possible to see where a conclusion is strong or weak.

Why it matters

DATOM optimizes decision credibility, serving stakeholders through speed, accountability, and safety.

For leadership

A clearer risk posture for high-stakes decisions ($50M-$200M).

For engineering

Faster iteration with fewer regressions by seeing which evidence supports a behavior.
